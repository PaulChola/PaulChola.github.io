About Me

I’m driven by curiosity and a deep desire to understand how things work. From a young age, I’ve always enjoyed asking questions, exploring systems, and learning by doing.

It’s funny how it all started. I remember it vividly — I must have been 18 or 19 at the time. Back then, I knew nothing about cyberspace. I was just curious, searching for tools to “hack systems” without really understanding what any of it meant. The first tools I ever downloaded were Metasploit and Nmap on my phone, which I later transferred to my laptop just to install them.

When I first installed Linux, I went around showing off to my friends. In reality, I was mostly watching movies on it. I had a powerful hacking operating system, but I didn’t really understand how it worked or how to use it. I didn’t know what ports or flags were I was just clicking through graphical interfaces, trying to make sense of things. That was around 2015.

By 2018, I had already killed three hard drives trying to install Kali Linux. Funny times. Even today, I’m still learning how everything works, and that’s the part I enjoy the most. The difference now is clarity. Back then, it was pure curiosity. Now, I understand what these tools are capable of and how to approach problems with purpose.

Almost ten years later, things are finally starting to make sense. I learn every day, build and deploy projects, and contribute wherever I can. This portfolio reflects that journey from curiosity to understanding, and from experimentation to growth.
